# MAXIMAULISM

A website hosting one author's essays and poems, styled in a
"schizo-INFOWARS-crusader-lightning-apocalypse" aesthetic. Built with Hugo +
TailwindCSS and hosted on GitHub Pages.

## Language

**MAXIMAULISM**:
The name of the website. Displayed as a large header on every page. Slogan:
"Life is sweetest near the bone".

**Rhetoric**:
The section containing the author's essays (prose).
_Avoid_: essays (as a label), articles, posts, blog.

**Eros**:
The section containing the author's poems (verse).
_Avoid_: poems (as a label), poetry.

**Fables**:
The section containing the author's fiction (stories). Currently has no pieces;
its section page shows a placeholder line ("the Imagination is hampered, the
fingers are sore, and the pen is broken") until content is added.
_Avoid_: fiction (as a nav label), stories, blog.

**Author**:
The single person whose essays and poems the site hosts (the requester's
friend). Distinct from the site owner/operator who builds and deploys it.
_Avoid_: user, contributor.

**The Crusader**:
The hero image on the main page — a lone crusader. Sourced from a public-domain
1843 portrait of Bohémond I of Antioch (Blondel), cropped to remove the name
plate, then given a warm candlelit CRT treatment (sepia, scanlines, flicker,
gold halo + drifting embers) in CSS. The glowing lightning eyes and lightning
bolts from the early prototypes were removed at the author's request. No AI
image generation is used; the Gemini key scaffolding (`.env.example`) is
retained but unused.
